We Don't Need No Education

08 Nov 2006

From an article on the Jacobs University Bremen (via German Joys), which recently changed its name after receiving a $250 million donations from a certain Herr Jacobs:

German universities lack the resources of their American rivals. The United States spends 2.6 percent of its gross domestic product on higher education; the Germans, only 1.1 percent.

I guess because Germany would rather spend money on the past (like coal subsidies) than on the future. The closing quote was also cute.

Why didnt Mr. Jacobs give his millions to his own alma mater, the University of Hamburg? They didnt ask.
